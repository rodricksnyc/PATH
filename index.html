FAQs

What is the PATH Study? ▾
The National Institutes of Health (NIH), in partnership with the Food and Drug Administration (FDA), is sponsoring an exciting national study on tobacco and health, called the PATH Study. This study includes people ages 12 and older, whether or not they use tobacco.
The study informs us about why and how people are using – or not using – different types of tobacco products (not just cigarettes) and how usage may affect health. Because we want to know why people do not use tobacco products, including tobacco non-users is critical. The PATH study has interviewed about 49,000 people at their homes, making it the largest study of its kind ever done in the United States.
Why was I selected? ▾
The PATH Study selected your address by chance from a list of addresses. Then the study selected adults and/or youth by chance from the individuals in your household. Whether or not you participate is up to you. However, the study results will be much more complete and useful if you participate.
Why should I be a part of this study? ▾
Whether or not you use tobacco (not just cigarettes), you will be an important part of what is learned about tobacco products and how they affect health. We think you will find the PATH Study interesting, and you will be performing a valuable public service.
How will you protect my privacy? ▾
The information you provide will be used for research purposes only and is kept private.
  • Your answers will not be connected with your name or other identifying information.
  • Your answers will be connected to a number only.
  • Your name and contact information will be kept separately in secure computer files.
What happens if I participate in this study? ▾
Adults in the study take part in an interview. Youth, 12 to 17 years old, will be asked to take part in an interview with parent permission. In some years, you may be asked to participate in other study activities.
The PATH Study is a longitudinal study, meaning we will request an interview from study participants every year or two.
