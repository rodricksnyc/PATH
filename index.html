FAQs

What is the PATH Study? ▾
The Population Assessment of Tobacco and Health (PATH), is a national study on tobacco use behavior and health. The study includes tobacco users and nonusers to better understand tobacco use behaviors and how attitudes about tobacco change over time.
What is the purpose of the PATH Study? ▾
The study is helping researchers understand why and how people are using, or not using, tobacco products. The study examines all tobacco products and not just cigarettes and how tobacco product use relates to health.
Who is conducting this study? ▾
The study is sponsored by the National Institutes of Health (NIH), in partnership with the Food and Drug Administration (FDA). The study is one of the largest of its kind and is conducted by Westat, an independent research firm for the NIH and the FDA.
Why was I selected/Why are you contacting me again? ▾
Contacting every person in the United States would be too expensive and time consuming, so you were selected to represent others in the U.S. The PATH Study is longitudinal, meaning that we follow–up with past participants like you, which helps us measure change in behaviors over time.
Do I have to participate in the PATH Study/Why should I continue to participate? ▾
You or your household’s participation in the PATH Study is completely voluntary. That means you may stop. However, your participation is very important and gives you a chance for your voice to be heard. Whether you or someone else in your household uses tobacco products or not, you are an important part of what we learn about the use of tobacco products in the U.S. You cannot be replaced and represent thousands of people like you – it is important that this study represents all types of people.
Why should my child participate? ▾
The PATH Study is learning about the health of people age 12 and older. Your child is an important part of the study. Hearing your child’s views and experiences each year helps to develop the best understanding of the use of tobacco products over time and how that may affect health. This information is important to those planning public policies for improving the health of Americans.
Who do I contact if I have other questions about the PATH Study? ▾
You can call PATH Study Support toll–free at 1-888-311-1819, or you can visit the PATH Study website at www.pathstudyinfo.nih.gov.
Will this information be kept confidential and safe? ▾
Your privacy is very important, and we take many steps to protect it. Your name or the names of any children participating are not linked to any information provided to the PATH Study. Results from this study are presented as totals for groups of people. No individual data is shared. The PATH Study is protected under Federal law 18 U.S.C. 1905, which prohibits federal employees from voluntarily disclosing a person’s identifying information or confidential statistical data.
